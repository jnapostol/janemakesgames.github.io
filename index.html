    Beary the Baker

    Summary

    The player plays as Beary the Baker. Beary is assigned the task of baking pumpkin pie for the big annual Fall Festival. He is missing a few ingredients and has to visit the local farm and supermarket to get what he needs. 
    The weather starts to change and gets colder. While collecting the ingredients, Beary notices the sudden change in weather. Beary encounters Santa on his way home to bake the pumpkin pie after getting all of the ingredients he needs Santa attempts to sabotage Beary by engaging in an altercation with him.

    Summary

    Post Mortem

    The Adventures of Puurcilla was the most recent game I created using Unity 2D. This was a project for my game-making course, DIG3713. This project didn't fully meet my expectations but I'm still proud of what I created and I thoroughly enjoyed the process. My biggest enemy was the amount of time I had to create my game. For instance, the story I had written for the game was too broad for the scope of the class since I only had one semester to work on the game.

    ​

     I realized developing solo was challenging because I was in charge of building the game with assets I had to either find or create. The experience I had prior to making this game helped build my understanding of what a level should have. The playthrough was pretty good but I struggled to find a good world-space camera because of how small I designed the character sprites.

    Post Mortem

    Detective Cupcake was my first-ever individually developed video game. This project was for the course, DIG2632. Thanks to this class, I realized that I wanted to pursue a career in making games and not just playing them. This project had greater scope than I realized during development. To find pieces of evidence I wanted players to use the mouse to click on areas on the screen. I also wanted the player to solve three mystery cases. In the end, I decided that to collect evidence, players have to enter a new area and talk to NPCs and complete one mystery case. 

    ​

    Unity was very new to me at the time and learning the software was rigorous. As previously mentioned, the scope of the game was large. I didn't realize how long it would take me to create all of the assets. Other functions and features I wanted in my game were also not within my scope because they required coding abilities, which I did not have at the time.
